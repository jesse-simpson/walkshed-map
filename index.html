📍 Walkshed Rezone by Property Type

v2-2026-03

Purpose

This tool identifies parcels in Seattle that would be upzoned under a proposed policy linking residential density to proximity to Major Transit Corridors in the One Seattle 2035 Comprehensive Plan.

It quantifies how many sub-LR2 parcels fall within 3-minute and 5-minute walksheds of the 18 designated Major Corridors, providing parcel-level data to support housing policy analysis and advocacy.

Policy Logic

Any parcel currently zoned below LR2 density within a corridor walkshed is flagged as an upzone candidate to LR2. The dataset contains two eligible zone types:

  • NR Neighborhood Residential — includes all pre-2023 NR1/NR2/NR3 designations and former SF by lot size and RSL zones, all consolidated under NR in the current dataset.
  • LR1 / LR1 RC Lowrise 1 — the lowest lowrise tier, one step below LR2.

Corridors

Analysis covers the 18 One Seattle 2035 (December 2025) Major Corridors only — Centers are excluded. Corridors can be toggled individually using the filter panel in the sidebar.

Walkshed Geometry

Walksheds are modeled as stadium buffers — a perpendicular slab along the corridor centerline with semicircular end-caps at each endpoint. Walk distances use a 1.3× network factor applied to straight-line radii:

WalkshedStraight-line radiusDisplay
3-min250 mBlue solid border
5-min416 mOrange dashed border

Each parcel is assigned to the nearest active corridor, then classified as 3-min or 5-min based on that distance. The 5-min count includes all parcels in both the inner and outer walkshed rings.

Caching

On first run, parcel data is fetched from ArcGIS and saved to localStorage. Subsequent runs load instantly from cache. Use Clear Cache in the sidebar to force a fresh fetch.

Address Lookup

Enter any Seattle address to check its current zone and whether it falls within a corridor walkshed. The tool reports the nearest corridor, walkshed distance (3-min or 5-min), and whether a proposed upzone applies.

Export

After analysis completes, use Export CSV to download all eligible parcels with fields: current zone, proposed zone (LR2), corridor name, address, lat/lng, and walkshed bucket.

Limitations

  • Corridor centerlines are approximated from One Seattle 2035 corridor designations, not surveyed right-of-way centerlines.
  • Walkshed geometry is a stadium buffer approximation, not a true pedestrian network analysis.
  • Parcel data reflects King County assessor records and may lag behind recent rezones by several months.
